Rage.

Taylor Swift's music occasionally touches on themes of anger or frustration, reflecting human emotions and experiences. However, her approach to expressing rage in her songs is more nuanced. Swift often channels these emotions into her lyrics, using them as a vehicle for storytelling or empowerment rather than solely dwelling on raw anger.
Songs like "Bad Blood" and "Look What You Made Me Do" hint at feelings of betrayal or frustration, but Swift typically infuses these emotions with a sense of resilience or empowerment. Her music tends to explore the complexities of emotions, showcasing a range of feelings, including anger, but often with a narrative that emphasizes growth, self-reflection, and strength in the face of challenges. Swift's approach to rage in her music is often part of a larger narrative that incorporates various emotions and personal growth within her storytelling.
